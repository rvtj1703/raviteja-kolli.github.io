About Me

Welcome to my website! I'm an enthusiastic Master's student at the University of Pennsylvania, specializing in Robotics. Currently, I work as a Research Assistant at the Figueroa Robotics Lab, affiliated with the GRASP Lab at UPenn. Within the realm of robotics, my primary interests encompass perception, robot control, and motion planning. These fields continuously captivate my curiosity, and I aspire to build a career dedicated to their exploration and advancement. My motivation stems from an unwavering passion to push the boundaries of technology and contribute to the ever-evolving landscape of robotics.

Outside of my academic pursuits, I find solace in music and enjoy sports such as cricket and badminton during my leisure time. Please feel free to connect with me. I'm always open to new opportunities and engaging conversations. Thank you for visiting my website, and I look forward to sharing and exploring the exciting world of robotics together!

Projects

Control of CrazyFlie quadrotor

In this project, my primary focus was enhancing quadrotor flight control and navigation. Employing advanced techniques including geometric nonlinear control, A* path planning, minimum-snap trajectories, Visual Inertial Odometry (VIO), and Error State Kalman Filter (ESKF), I achieved a significant 25% reduction in average flight time across six diverse maps. This reduction was achieved by comparing against constant velocity trajectories without VIO assistance and minimum snap trsjectories with VIO assistance.

NeRF: Neural Radiance Field in 3D Vision

With guidance from Prof. Kostas Daniilidis, I implemented Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) for LEGO model rendering, achieving a PSNR of 25.17. This project builds on K. Gao et al.'s research.
